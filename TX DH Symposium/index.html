Core Role Responsibilities

The Moderator Role

As the "Face" of the Block, your job is to guide the presenters and the audience through the schedule with a warm, confident, and professional presence.

  • Lead Communications: Deliver all scripted announcements, introduce presenters, and facilitate all Q&A sessions.
  • Manage the "Backstage": Greet presenters in the pre-session, confirm their details, and run tech checks to ensure they are comfortable and ready.
  • Keep the Show Moving: Announce breaks, manage transitions between speakers, and keep an eye on the clock to ensure the block stays on schedule.

The Tech Support Role

As the "Director" of the Block, you are the technical backbone of the meeting, ensuring a smooth and seamless experience for everyone.

  • Own the Controls: Your first step is to ensure you have full Host controls in Zoom.
  • Run the Tech: Verify all Zoom settings, manage the waiting room, start/stop the recording, and control the countdown timer.
  • Manage Permissions: You are in charge of assigning and removing co-host status for presenters and ensuring they are muted when not presenting.
  • Support the Q&A: Handle the technical side of the Q&A, such as unmuting participants who have their hands raised.
  • Be the Safety Net: Monitor the chat for any attendees experiencing technical difficulties and be the first line of defense for any unexpected issues.
