Core Role Responsibilities

The Moderator Role: The "Face" of the Session

  • Be the Host: You are the master of ceremonies. Your primary job is to guide the presenters and the audience through the session with a warm, confident, and professional presence.
  • Lead Communications: Deliver all scripted announcements, introduce the presenters, and facilitate the Q&A sessions.
  • Manage the "Backstage": Greet presenters in the pre-session, confirm their details, and run the tech checks to ensure they are comfortable and ready.
  • Keep the Show Moving: Announce breaks, manage transitions between speakers, and keep an eye on the clock to ensure the session stays on schedule.

The Tech Support Role: The "Director" of the Session

  • Own the Controls: You are the technical backbone of the meeting. Your first step is to ensure you have full Host controls in Zoom.
  • Run the Tech: Verify all Zoom settings, manage the waiting room, start/stop the recording, and control the countdown timer.
  • Manage Permissions: You are in charge of assigning and removing co-host status for presenters as needed.
  • Support the Q&A: You will handle the technical side of the Q&A, such as unmuting participants who have their hands raised.
  • Be the Safety Net: Monitor the chat for any attendees experiencing technical difficulties and be the first line of defense for any unexpected issues.
